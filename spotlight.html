Pennsylvania | Fallingwater
Image of building
Architect: Frank Lloyd Wright (1867-1959)
Year: 1935
Location: Outside of Pittsburgh
Fallingwater is a house designed for the Kaufmann family, owners of Pittsburgh’s largest department store. One of Wright’s most widely acclaimed works, Fallingwater exemplifies his philosophy of organic architecture: the harmonious union of art and nature.
Fallingwater is owned and operated by the Conservancy and open to the public to tour as a museum. It is surrounded by 5,100 acres of natural land, streams and trails known as the Bear Run Nature Reserve. On July 10, 2019, UNESCO inscribed Fallingwater and seven other Frank Lloyd Wright-designed buildings to the World Heritage List. Fallingwater is the only major Wright work to come into the public domain with its setting, artwork and original Wright-designed furnishings intact.
